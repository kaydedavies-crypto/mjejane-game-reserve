History & Heritage

A Name Rooted in
Living History

The name Mjejane honours Chief Mjejane, who in 1828 led his people, many of them Tsonga, southward out of present-day Mozambique, fleeing the turbulence that followed the conquests of Zulu King Shaka. Chief Mjejane's group settled along the banks of the Crocodile River: the very ground on which this reserve stands today.

Situated in Mpumalanga along the Crocodile River, Mjejane Private Game Reserve lies directly between the Malelane and Crocodile Bridge gates of the Kruger National Park. It holds a distinction shared by no other private reserve in South Africa, exclusive low-level bridge access that connects directly into the southern Kruger.

This is Big Five wilderness, lion, leopard, elephant, rhino, and buffalo roam freely across the reserve and into Kruger beyond. The Crocodile River corridor draws exceptional predator density, prolific birdlife, and the kind of raw, unscripted wildlife encounters that cannot be manufactured elsewhere.

How You'll Spend Your Days

Experiences That
Stay With You

Beyond the ordinary safari, curated encounters that immerse you in the landscape and its wildlife, guided by rangers who have spent lifetimes reading the bush.

01

Dawn & Dusk
Game Drives

Venture deep into Big Five territory aboard an open 4×4 at first light or last fire. Our senior rangers and trackers read the land with instincts sharpened over decades, every drive is unrepeatable.

02

Guided
Bush Walks

Read the land on foot with an armed field guide. Discover the hidden world beneath the grass, tracks, insects, medicinal plants, the intimate details a vehicle will never reveal.

03

Birding
Expeditions

With over 350 bird species recorded along the Crocodile River corridor, Mjejane is a birdwatcher's paradise. From the African Fish Eagle to the rare Pels Fishing Owl, the skies here astonish.

04

Sundowner
Experiences

As the bush cools and shadows lengthen, pause at a curated viewpoint with sundowners and canapés as the Lowveld sun paints the sky in shades of amber, copper, and fire.

05

Night Drive
Safari

The African night belongs to a different cast entirely. A spotlight-guided drive reveals leopard, civets, porcupines, and the raw, unscripted drama of nocturnal predators on the hunt.

06

Bush
Dining

Dine under a canopy of stars at a lantern-lit boma, or take breakfast at the water's edge as the bush wakes around you. Every meal celebrates the finest South African produce and bold bush flavours.

07

Conservation
& Legacy

Engage with our conservation team and learn about rhino protection, anti-poaching efforts, and the ecological stewardship that keeps Mjejane wild, and will continue to do so for generations to come.
